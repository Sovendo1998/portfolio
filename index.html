About Me

Hi, my name is Sovendo Talapatra. I recently received my undergraduate degree from the department of Materials & Metallurgical Engineering at Bangladesh University of Engineering & technology. Over the last year of my undergrad, I was heavily involved with several computational research projects in the field of photovoltaics and energy storage. These projects were carried out through collaboration with the research group of Dr. Saquib Ahmed from SUNY Buffalo University. I modelled & investigated thin film perovskite solar devices through numerical simulation, density functional theory & machine learning. I also collaborated on a molecular dynamics simulation of bilayer silicene anode with Mg-ion intercalation. My work on these projects was driven by a passion for exploring the intersection between material science & renewable energy.During my post-graduate studies, I wish to continue to investigate these material systems & expand my research through first principles/data-driven computational studies along with experimental synthesis and characterization techniques. I have a keen interest in exploring stability and degradation of these types of materials/devices and work towards removing barriers to their commercialization.

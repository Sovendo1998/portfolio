About Me

Hello, my name is Sovendo Talapatra, a graduate in Materials and Metallurgical Engineering from Bangladesh Unversity abd Technology (BUET). From the final years of my undergrad, I worked as a research assistant in Nanotechnology and Thin Film Lab under the supervision of Dr. Md. Muktadir Billah. My main focus was on optimizing the thin film synthesis process using the sol-gel spin coating route for optoelectronic applications. I also gained hands-on experience in nanoparticle synthesis routes such as chemical co-precipitation, sol-gel, and hydrothermal. The purpose of these nanoparticle syntheses was to study their photocatalytic activity, antimicrobial activity, and electrochemical applications, and how these properties varied due to the incorporation of doping elements. I am passionate about exploring the manipulation of engineering properties of atoms and molecules at the nanoscale and I am keenly interested in exploring the implications of nanotechnology in optoelectronic devices, integrated photonics, and photovoltaics. Furthermore, I find the science of materials characterization tools fascinating in figuring out the fundamentals of materials science from laboratory scales to industrial manufacturing levels.
